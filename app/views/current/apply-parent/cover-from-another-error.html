Healthcare cover from another country

This may be an S1, E121, E106, or E109 to cover your health costs in the UK. For example, you've previously lived in another country outside the UK and you're receiving a state pension or benefit from that country.

If you're temporarily studying in the UK but normally live in another country, you may have a health insurance card from that country to cover your healthcare costs while you are studying.

What is an S1, E121, E106, or E109?

An S1, E121, E106, or E109 certificate issued by an EU country, Iceland, Norway, Liechtenstein, or Switzerland for a person living in the UK entitles them to access the NHS in the same way as a UK resident. You may be entitled to one of these certificates if you:

  • receive a qualifying benefit or state pension from one of the above countries, and do not receive a UK State Pension
  • are a frontier worker (someone who lives in the UK and works in one of those countries)
  • are assessed by the issuing country that you are entitled to a certificate for another reason
  • are the family member of someone who has been awarded an S1, E121, or E106 certificate

Answer yes if you:

  • are entitled to an S1, E121, E106, or E109 certificate even if one has not been issued to you
  • have been provided with an S1, E121, E106, or E109 certificate that you have not yet registered with the NHS
  • have an S1, E121, E106, or E109 certificate which is registered with the NHS
